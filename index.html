About me

Biomedical AI researcher working with complex physiological data.

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Center for Mind/Brain Sciences, University of Trento, working at the intersection of machine learning, biomedical signal processing, and neuroAI.

My research focuses on developing robust and interpretable models for complex physiological signals, with a particular interest in EEG, fMRI, multimodal representation learning, and brain decoding. I completed my PhD at IMT Atlantique, where I worked on clinical neuroimaging, post-stroke rehabilitation, neurofeedback, and reproducible health-data pipelines.

More broadly, I am interested in transforming noisy biomedical data into reliable, actionable indicators for neuroscience, healthcare, and connected health applications.

Research interests

From noisy biomedical signals to reliable machine-learning systems.

Clinical neuroimaging

Multimodal learning

Learning shared representations across EEG, fMRI, speech, images, and language using modern deep-learning methods.
